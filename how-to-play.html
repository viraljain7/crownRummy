The Objective & Core Concepts

The goal is to arrange your 13 cards into valid sets and sequences. A winning hand must have at least two sequences, one of which must be a pure sequence.

Forming Sequences

Pure Sequence: 3+ consecutive cards of the same suit (no Joker).

The Gameplay Loop


  1. Deal & Toss

    13 cards are dealt to each player. A random card determines the Wild Joker for the round, and another toss decides the first player.

  2. Draw

    On your turn, begin by drawing one card from either the face-down Closed Deck or the face-up Open Deck (discard pile).

  3. Discard

    After drawing, you must end your turn by discarding one of your 14 cards to the Open Deck.

  4. Declare

    Once your hand is arranged into valid melds, discard your final card to declare your win. An invalid declaration results in a penalty.

Point Calculation


Card Values

  • A, K, Q, J : 10 Points
  • 10, 9, 8...2 : Face Value
  • Joker : 0 Points

Winner's Score

The player who makes a valid declaration receives zero points.

Loser's Score

Losing players accumulate points equal to the sum of their cards that are not part of a valid meld.

Wrong Declaration

A player making an invalid declaration is penalized with 80 points.

Game Variants

Master the core game, then test your skills in our different Rummy formats.

Points Rummy

The fastest format, ideal for quick games. Winnings are based on the points accumulated by opponents in a single deal.

Pool Rummy

A game of elimination. Players are knocked out upon reaching a point threshold (101/201). Last player standing wins.

Deals Rummy

A strategic format played for a fixed number of deals. The player with the most chips at the end is the winner.

Glossary of Terms

Meld
A valid group of cards, either a sequence or a set.
Pure Sequence
A sequence formed without a Joker. A minimum of one is mandatory to win.
Impure Sequence
A sequence where one or more cards are replaced by a Joker.
Set
A group of 3 or 4 cards of the same rank but different suits.
Wild Joker
A card randomly chosen at the start of a round. All cards of that rank also become Jokers.
Drop
To exit a game round early for a fixed penalty, if you have a weak hand.
